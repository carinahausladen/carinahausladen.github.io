Collaboration with arcos Gallo and Carina I Hausladen and Ming Hsu and Adrianna Jenkins and Colin Camerer

<u>Abstract:</u>
Discrimination during the hiring process can have serious consequences for both individuals and society at large. In this study, we investigate the perceptual factors that may contribute to such discrimination by analyzing data from a meta-analytic sample of correspondence papers.
Our results demonstrate that discrimination is present across a range of groups, with significant disparities observed in hiring rates for certain demographic categories. We also explore the role of warmth and competence perceptions in predicting discrimination, finding that perceptions of friendliness and amicability can have a particularly strong influence on hiring decisions.
