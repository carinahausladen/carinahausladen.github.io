Text-to-image Models  | Artificial Intelligence | Causal Fairness Analysis | Bias | Stereotyping


Bias in AI, particularly in large generative systems like CLIP, demands a sophisticated understanding for effective mitigation. 
While many studies highlight the issue, they often lack a foundational theoretical model. 
Drawing from social psychology, this study adapts established theories on human stereotyping to quantify bias in text-to-image models. 
By analyzing the cosine similarity between text and image embeddings, we assess the model's bias using two datasets: a novel one for causal stereotype examination and the benchmark 'fairface' for robustness validation.
Our innovative methodology utilizes a Generative Adversarial Network (GAN) dataset, where seeds manipulated in latent space represent varying race and gender combinations. 
Surprisingly, findings suggest Asians and females are perceived more warmly and competently, challenging some social psychology theories and offering fresh insights on AI bias.
