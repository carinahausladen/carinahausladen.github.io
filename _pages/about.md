Welcome to my homepage! I am a postdoctoral researcher working with Colin Camerer at Caltech, exploring the dynamics of stereotype perceptions in the labor market. My research interests are diverse and lie at the intersection of behavioral economics, law and economics, neuroeconomics, social choice theory, computational social science, and circadian rhythms.

I earned my Ph.D. in Economics from the University of Cologne and the Max Planck Institute for Research on Collective Goods, where I focused on leveraging machine learning tools to gain insights into human decision-making in economic contexts. I was fortunate to be supervised by Prof. Dr. Christoph Engel and Prof. Dr. Martin Fochmann. Before that, I obtained a Master's degree in Economics from the University of Passau and a Bachelor's degree in Business from the University of Augsburg.

As a postdoctoral researcher at the Computational Social Science department at ETH Zurich, I expanded my research vision and explored a range of fascinating topics. Among my most notable research endeavors were investigations into participatory resilience, futuristic traffic paradigms, and social choice theory to optimize voting mechanisms. My research experience also includes various research internships and assistant positions, such as my work at the Psychophysiology Laboratory at Stanford University, where I studied the theory of value-based decision making.

My current research interests revolve around the fascinating field of chronobiology and its potential applications in understanding human behavior. Along with a dear friend and colleague from ETHZ, I recently proposed a theoretical framework that explores how circadian rhythms and neural synchrony shape networked cooperation. Additionally, another colleague and I are currently working on a study exploring how chronobiology can be leveraged to combat the spread of online disinformation. If you share similar research interests and are excited about exploring the potential of chronobiology in understanding human behavior, please don't hesitate to reach out. I am always eager to collaborate and exchange ideas with fellow researchers.

In addition to my academic pursuits, I am a sports enthusiast and enjoy various outdoor activities, including swimming, road cycling, and trail running. The stunning beauty of the Swiss mountains in winter inspired me to try cross-country skiing, and I immediately fell in love with the sport.  

Playing the harp is another of my greatest passions, and I dedicate a significant amount of time to practicing for performances with symphony and chamber orchestras. I have had the privilege of playing in concerts and events across multiple continents and at professional concert halls. Recordings of some of my past performances can be found in the Harp-tap.
