<u>Abstract:</u>
We introduce a modified version of the die-in-the-cup paradigm to study (dis-)honest behavior under time pressure. Replacing the regular die with one that has a distinct color on either side enables us to manipulate the amount of familiarity with the randomization device.This removes the limitations of the original paradigm and allows fora test of theories that suggest that (dis-)honest behavior is affected by the relative difficulty of generating false reports. We also replace the cup with a simple mechanical device for better control over the process of rolling the die and collected mouse movement data from the participants to investigate the present behavioral archetypes. Ourmain finding is that time pressure leads to more dishonest behavior,but only if the regular die is used. We also find that when given thetime to deliberate, the participants generally report lower values if the standard rather than the color die is used.
